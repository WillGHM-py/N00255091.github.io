Advice (Exercise)

It's imporatnt to keep in mind this is general health advice and it is recommended to do what you feel is best for you and what will help you the most to reach your own goals if you're BMI is above the normal range, which is 18.5 - 25 it can be viewed as unhealthy, but BMI is measured by weight over height squared this means that someone with a high muscle Mass be seen as obese even if they are healthy this is why it's important to keep that in mind when using BMI. if you are over the average range for BMI it would be recommended to consider going into a calorie defeciet and if you are under the normal BMI range it is reommended to go into a calorie surplus, the two categories your calories to maintain, gain or lose half a kilo a week I recommend starting slow and increasing the surplus or deficit incremenetly aswell as increasing exercise amounts with this. If you jump into the deep end and do too much at once, it is unlikely to stick. The goal isn’t for short-term gain but to create new and healthy habits, the recommended daily exercise is 150 minutes of moderate intensity exercise a week. This is recommended for everyone and is well doable for most, although it can sound daunting, it is the most sure way to live healthily.

Advice (Dietary)

Another important factor to keep in mind is diet. Diet can be quite complicated, so i’ll try to keep it simple, I’ll split it into three groups: Carbs, Fat, and Proteins. Carbs can be very complicated as they can be split into Complex carbs and Refined Carbs. Complex Carbs could be viewed as good and Refined Carbs as bad. Examples of Refined Carbs are white bread, cereals, and things of that sort, while complex carbs are like brown rice, brown bread, and high-fiber foods. If you are wanting to lose weight, it would be recommended to lessen refined carb intake and sugary foods, and increase protein intake, as it’s more filling, if you want to lose weight, eat more food, and increase protein intake if you want to build muscle.
